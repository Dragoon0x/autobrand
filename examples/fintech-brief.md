# Brand Brief: Clearpath

## What is this?

**Name:** Clearpath
**One-liner:** Payments infrastructure for businesses that move fast.
**Domain:** B2B fintech, payment processing, developer tools

## Who is it for?

Engineering-led startups that need reliable payments without the enterprise sales pitch. CTOs and lead developers who pick tools based on documentation quality, not slide decks. They value speed, clarity, and things that work the first time.

## What should it feel like?

- Trustworthy without being corporate. Banks feel trustworthy through heaviness. This should feel trustworthy through clarity.
- Technical confidence. Like well-written documentation. Clean, precise, no wasted words.
- Modern but not trendy. Five years from now this should still look current.
- Fast. Everything about the brand should feel like speed. Light pages, sharp type, zero friction.

## What should it NEVER feel like?

- Banking. No navy blue, no serif logos, no shield icons.
- Generic SaaS. No gradient buttons, no "trusted by 10,000+ companies" without substance.
- Cold. Technical doesn't mean robotic. There should be warmth in the precision.
- Playful. This handles money. Whimsy is wrong.

## Color direction

Cool but not cold. Think polished steel, clean glass, morning light. A palette that feels engineered. One accent for actions and success states. Green is obvious for money. Be smarter than that.

## Typography direction

A sans-serif with a technical spine. Not geometric (too cold). Not humanist (too warm). Something that looks great in code snippets and in headlines. Mono for code, naturally.

## Voice and tone

Direct. Technical vocabulary is fine when it's the right word. Never use jargon to impress. Write documentation-quality prose: clear enough that you never need to re-read a sentence.

## Hard constraints

- Logo must work at 16x16 favicon size
- WCAG AA contrast ratios
- Palette works in light and dark mode
- No more than 2 typefaces plus a monospace
- SVG logomark under 5KB
