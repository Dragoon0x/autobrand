# Brand Brief: Origin Line

## What is this?

**Name:** Origin Line
**One-liner:** Single-origin coffee roasted with intention.
**Domain:** Specialty coffee, direct trade, small-batch roasting

## Who is it for?

People who notice the difference between good coffee and great coffee. Not snobs. Just people who care. They shop at farmers markets, read ingredient labels, and choose quality over convenience. They want to know where their coffee comes from and who grew it.

## What should it feel like?

- Grounded. Like holding a warm ceramic mug in the morning. Physical, real, present.
- Craft without pretension. The kind of quality that doesn't need to announce itself.
- Earthy and honest. Nothing polished to the point of losing character.
- Small-batch energy. This is not a chain. It should feel like a place you discover, not a brand that discovers you.

## What should it NEVER feel like?

- Corporate coffee. No Starbucks energy. No rewards programs or app-first thinking.
- Hipster parody. No ironic typography, no beards-and-fixies aesthetic.
- Luxury. This is craft, not lifestyle aspiration. No gold foil, no marble textures.
- Minimalist to the point of bland. There should be texture and warmth, not sterile white space.

## Color direction

Earth tones. Soil, bark, clay, dried leaves. Think about the colors you see in a coffee farm, not a coffee shop. Brown is obvious but do it well. One warmer accent for energy, maybe a deep amber or burnt orange.

## Typography direction

Something with history for the name. A typeface that could work on a burlap sack or a ceramic label. Not script. Not geometric. Something that has weight and texture. Body text should be readable and friendly. No mono.

## Voice and tone

Warm, knowledgeable, never condescending. Talk about coffee the way a good friend who happens to know a lot about coffee talks about coffee. Specific but not technical. "This one has notes of brown sugar and dried cherry" not "cupping score 87.5."

## Hard constraints

- Logo must work at 16x16 and on packaging
- WCAG AA contrast ratios
- Palette should feel at home on kraft paper and on screen
- No more than 2 typefaces
- SVG logomark under 5KB
