# Brand Brief: Form & Matter

## What is this?

**Name:** Form & Matter
**One-liner:** A studio at the intersection of design and engineering.
**Domain:** Creative technology, product design, interactive installations

## Who is it for?

Brands and cultural institutions that need something built that hasn't been built before. Art directors, museum curators, creative directors at agencies who have a vision but need a team that can think and build at the same level. They hire for capability and taste, not price.

## What should it feel like?

- Precise and intentional. Every choice visible and defensible.
- The confidence of a well-curated gallery. Nothing competing for attention, everything earning its place.
- Editorial restraint. More Monocle than Wired.
- Slightly austere but not cold. The warmth comes from the quality of execution, not from softness.

## What should it NEVER feel like?

- Agency flashy. No dark mode with neon accents, no "we make award-winning digital experiences" energy.
- Portfolio factory. Not a grid of thumbnails with hover effects.
- Academic or conceptual. The work is real and the brand should feel that way.
- Cute or approachable. This studio charges premium rates and the brand should reflect that without being pretentious about it.

## Color direction

Nearly monochrome. Black, white, and one color that makes the whole thing feel alive. The single color should be unexpected. Not blue, not red. Something that makes you look twice.

## Typography direction

One typeface, used exceptionally well. Weight, size, and spacing doing all the work. If you need two typefaces, you're not using the first one well enough. Mono is acceptable for functional elements but should feel like a choice, not a default.

## Voice and tone

Sparse. Sentences that do exactly one thing and do it well. No adjectives that aren't earning their keep. The kind of copy where removing a word would make it worse, not better. Think gallery wall text, not marketing copy.

## Hard constraints

- Logo must work at 16x16 favicon size
- WCAG AA contrast ratios
- Must work on white and black backgrounds equally well
- One typeface maximum (plus optional mono for functional use)
- SVG logomark under 3KB (simplicity is the constraint)
